1.5 Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 protects original creations involving works of literary, music, art, film and sound The Copyright (Computer Programs) Regulations 1992 extends protection to software. As soon as you post to your website it is automatically copyrighted under these laws. When inserting images into a website it is important to make sure they are Copyright and Royalty free. There are a number of different websites that offer royalty free images I used lostandtaken.com Reference https://www.termsfeed.com/blog/website-copyright-law/#U_K
1.6

UK Law states that a website should be made accessible to disabled users. If your website does not confirm to certain design standards you could be sued for discrimination. The Royal National Institute for the Blind has initiated legal action against companies in 2 cases. Companies need to make sure they are not discriminating against the blind as any court case action could damage their reputation. Visually impaired people can use a screen reader to translate the contents of web pages using speech synthesisers or Braille displays. If alt attributes are not added to all images tags the user would find it hard to understand what is on the page. Reference https://www.pinsentmasons.com/out-law/guides/disabled-access-to-websites-under-uk-law

1.7

Standard file formats for websites include HTML - Hypertext markup language almost all websites are written in HTML. JPG - Joint Photographic experts group file, this is a 16 bit format capable of displaying millions of colours at once and is designed for photographs PNG - Portable Network Graphics, PNG files are useful for web applications as you can use alpha channels to use effects such as translucent or opaque. PDF - Portable document format, PDF files are useful as documents can be transferred and viewed on different computers and operating systems without the need for any special software other than a free PDF viewer.
